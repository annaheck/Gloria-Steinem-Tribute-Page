Gloria Steinem

Gloria Steinem was an American feminist and journalist. She was born March 25, 1934 in Toledo, Ohio. She graduated from Smith College, Magna Cum Laude. In 1962, she wrote an article about contraception for Esquire Magazine, her first "serious" assignment. Then in 1963 while working for Show magazine, she wrote an article exposing the conditions and treatment of women at the New York Playboy club (where she was working). After that in 1972, she founded Ms. Magazine. Following that, she became the first woman to speak at the National Press Conference. During her career as a journalist, she also played a large role as an activist. In 1969 she wrote the article "After Black Power, Women Liberation," which brought her to fame in the feminist community. In 1970, she campaigned for Equal Rights Amendment, and also wrote an article for time "What Would it be Like If Women Win." Her work in feminism and journalism helped women get to where they are today and influenced thousands across the world.
